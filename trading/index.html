What should I actually do?

In plain words

1 · Today: do nothing. All three warning lights are green. Keep the index fund, keep the plan. Boredom is the correct feeling.

2 · Know your tripwire in advance. If SPY finishes a month below ~$693 (it's at 738), the first light turns red — the tested response is to cut stock exposure roughly in half. Two lights red: get defensive. This is decided now, calmly, not during the scary week it happens.

3 · Don't short. Don't chase. Betting on declines has no evidence behind it in our tests (why), and buying more just because the market is up isn't a signal either. The system's whole job is: stay in, until the specific tripwires say otherwise.

What that stance means

  • Longs: hold. The base rate is on your side (markets rose in ~64% of all months in the sample) and no tested warning is active. See chapter 4 for why "no warning" is the long signal.
  • Shorts: no. Even the scariest tested condition (credit stress + broken trend) still averaged positive next-month returns historically. The evidence supports de-risking in storms — it does not yet support betting on declines. The full argument is in chapter 4.
  • New purchases: the rules are indifferent to timing here — they say the environment is normal. What they do say: know in advance that the de-risk line sits near $693 on SPY.

The path to an investing agent

The end state is an agent that watches, reasons, and proposes — with you approving trades until it has earned more rope. Each stage must prove itself before the next unlocks.

  • Stage 0 — Research system (you are here). Signals, backtests, and the evidence graph, rebuilt on demand. The agent's future brain.
  • Stage 1 — Watcher. Daily cron: refresh signals, alert on any flip via the morning brief / WhatsApp. No opinions, just tripwires. One command away.
  • Stage 2 — Analyst. On each flip or weekly, the agent writes a short memo: what changed, what the tested edges say, what it would do and why — every claim linked to a graph edge. You do the clicking.
  • Stage 3 — Trader with training wheels. The agent proposes exact orders (paper-traded first, then the empty Agentic account) under hard guardrails: only rules with validated edges, position caps, no shorts without a validated negative edge, full decision log.
  • Stage 4 — Autonomy, earned. Only after the decision log shows the agent following its own rules through at least one full regime change. Trust is a backtest too.
